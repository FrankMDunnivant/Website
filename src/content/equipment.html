Laboratory Equipment and Instrumentation

The environmental chemistry research laboratory at Whitman College is equipped with modern analytical instrumentation for environmental analysis and research.

Major Instrumentation

  • GC-MS - Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry
  • ICP-MS - Inductively Coupled Plasma-Mass Spectrometry
  • CE System - Capillary Electrophoresis
  • HPLC - High Performance Liquid Chromatography with UV-Vis and Fluorescence detectors
  • AA Spectrometers - Flame and Graphite Furnace Atomic Absorption Spectrometers
  • GC-ECD - Gas Chromatography with Electron Capture Detection
  • Ion Chromatograph
  • TOC Analyzer - Total Organic Carbon Analyzer

Research Capabilities

  • Trace-level pollutant analysis in aquatic systems
  • Isotopic analysis of metals
  • Sample preparation and extraction techniques
  • Environmental monitoring and assessment
  • PCB and pesticide analysis
  • Heavy metal determination
  • Headspace solid-phase microextraction (HS-SPME)

Instrumentation Funding

Major instrumentation has been acquired through competitive grants including:

  • M.J. Murdock Charitable Trust - $182,500 plus $120,000 matching (GC-MS and CE system)
  • Camille and Henry Dreyfus Foundation - $50,000 matching for ICP-MS
  • Government Surplus - Over $200,000 in equipment from U.S. EPA and DOE
